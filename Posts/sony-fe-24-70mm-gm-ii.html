Chromatic Aberration Control

Lateral chromatic aberration is exceptionally well controlled. Even in extreme high-contrast situationsâ€”shooting tree branches against bright sky, for exampleâ€”there's minimal color fringing. Distortion and Vignetting

At 24mm, there's moderate barrel distortion that's automatically corrected by in-camera processing or in RAW conversion software. With correction applied (which happens automatically in most workflows), lines remain straight without wavy artifacts. Sony's approach of allowing moderate optical distortion but correcting it digitally enables better overall optical performance elsewhere.

Vignetting at F2.8 is noticeable in RAW files but typically helps direct attention to the subject center-frame. It's easily corrected if needed, or embraced as part of the rendering character. By F4, vignetting becomes minimal.

Flare and Ghosting Resistance

Sony's Nano AR II coating delivers outstanding flare resistance. Shooting directly into the sun produces well-controlled flares without veiling or significant contrast loss. There's the occasional well-defined ghost or flare element, but it's predictable and often visually appealing rather than image-destroying.

In backlighting scenarios, the lens maintains excellent contrast and color saturation. You can shoot into light sources without the washed-out look that ruins images from lesser lenses. This capability expands creative possibilities significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the GM II worth the upgrade from the original GM?

For professionals, especially those doing video work, yes. The lighter weight, faster autofocus, reduced focus breathing, and improved corners represent meaningful improvements. For enthusiasts, the original GM remains excellent; upgrade only if the specific improvements matter to your work.

How does this compare to prime lenses for image quality?

The GM II delivers image quality remarkably close to equivalent prime lenses. While F1.4 primes still offer shallower depth-of-field and potentially slightly better resolution, the difference is much smaller than previous zoom generations. For most applications, image quality differences become negligible.

Can I use this lens for landscape photography?

Absolutely. The corner-to-corner sharpness, minimal chromatic aberration, and excellent flare resistance make it capable for landscape work. The 24mm wide end covers many landscape needs. For ultra-wide perspectives, you'd need a dedicated wide-angle lens, but this handles most landscape shooting excellently.

Does the front element rotate during focusing?

Yes, which complicates polarizing filter use. You may need to refocus then adjust the polarizer rather than adjusting while looking through the viewfinder. For other filters, rotation doesn't matter. Some photographers consider this a notable disadvantage for landscape work with polarizers.

How is the autofocus for fast-moving subjects?

Exceptional. Paired with Sony's latest bodies featuring AI-based subject detection, the lens tracks running children, athletes, and wildlife reliably. The lightweight focus elements and four XD Linear motors enable rapid focus adjustments without hunting or pulsing.

Is the lens sharp enough for 61-megapixel sensors?

Absolutely. The lens resolves detail beautifully even on Sony's highest-resolution sensors. There's no optical limitation preventing you from utilizing full sensor resolution. The lens is clearly designed for current and future high-megapixel bodies.
