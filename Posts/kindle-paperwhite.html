Frequently Asked Questions

Can I read library books on the Kindle Paperwhite?

Yes! Most public libraries support OverDrive/Libby, allowing you to borrow e-books for free. Simply link your library card, browse available titles, and send them directly to your Kindle.

Does the Kindle Paperwhite have a touchscreen?

Yes, the entire display is a responsive touchscreen. Tap to turn pages, swipe to access menus, and use gestures for navigation. It's intuitive and works even with slightly damp fingers.

Can I read PDFs on the Kindle Paperwhite?

Absolutely. Email PDFs to your Kindle address or transfer via USB-C. PDFs can be reflowed for better reading, though complex layouts may work better on larger screens.

Is Kindle Unlimited worth it?

If you read 2+ books monthly, yes. At $11.99/month, it pays for itself quickly. The selection includes bestsellers, new releases, and classics. Try the free trial to see if it fits your reading habits.

How do I get free books for my Kindle?

Multiple ways: Project Gutenberg offers 70,000+ free classics, Amazon's "Top 100 Free" list updates daily, Prime Reading is free for Prime members, and library e-books via OverDrive/Libby are completely free.
