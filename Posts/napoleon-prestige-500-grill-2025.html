Infrared Rear Burner and Rotisserie System

One of the Prestige 500's standout features is its infrared rear burner and included rotisserie system:

How Infrared Technology Works

Traditional burners heat air, which then heats food. Infrared burners emit intense radiant heat directly to food, similar to the sun warming your skin. This produces incredibly even heating, higher temperatures, and better searing with less fuel consumption.

Rear Infrared Burner Benefits

  • 18,000 BTU Output: Powerful infrared burner provides intense, even heat across the entire rotisserie cooking area.
  • Perfect for Rotisserie: Consistent heat from back to front ensures even browning and cooking of rotating meats.
  • Energy Efficient: Infrared technology uses less fuel while producing more heat than conventional burners.
  • Reduced Flare-Ups: Radiant heat doesn't rely on air movement, minimizing grease-fueled flare-ups.
  • Superior Browning: Intense radiant energy creates gorgeous, evenly browned exteriors on roasted meats.

Professional Rotisserie System

Napoleon includes a complete commercial-grade rotisserie kit—not a cheap add-on, but a robust system designed for serious use:

  • Heavy-Duty Motor: Powerful rotisserie motor handles up to 50 pounds—whole turkeys, prime rib roasts, multiple chickens simultaneously.
  • Stainless Steel Spit Rod: Thick stainless spit rod with adjustable forks secures even heavy, irregular cuts of meat.
  • Counterbalance System: Included counterbalance ensures smooth, vibration-free rotation even with uneven loads.
  • Easy Mounting: Spit rod slides into dedicated brackets inside the grill—no fumbling with awkward attachments.
  • Professional Results: Slow rotation over infrared heat produces juicy, evenly cooked meats with crispy, golden skin rivaling high-end restaurants.

Innovative WAVE Cooking Grids

Napoleon's signature WAVE cooking grids are more than just aesthetic—they're engineered for superior grilling performance:

Design and Function

  • Unique Wave Pattern: The undulating wave design creates multiple contact points with food for distinctive diamond-pattern sear marks.
  • Self-Basting Channels: Valleys in the wave pattern collect juices and fats, continuously basting food as it cooks for enhanced moisture and flavor.
  • Heavy-Gauge Construction: Thick stainless steel retains heat exceptionally well for restaurant-quality searing.
  • Optimal Spacing: Grid spacing prevents small items from falling through while still allowing efficient heat transfer.
  • Easy Cleaning: Stainless steel surface releases food residue easily—simple brushing after use keeps grids clean.

Sear Marks That Impress

The WAVE pattern creates those impressive crosshatch grill marks you see at high-end steakhouses. But beyond aesthetics, those marks represent proper caramelization—the Maillard reaction that develops complex flavors and appetizing crust. The wave design ensures consistent, even contact across the food surface, producing better browning than flat grates.

Maintenance and Care

The Prestige 500's stainless construction simplifies maintenance while ensuring longevity:

Routine Cleaning

  • Cooking Grids: Brush while hot after each use; deep clean every few months with grill cleaner
  • Heat Tents: Remove and clean annually to remove accumulated carbon buildup
  • Drip Tray: Empty and clean every few uses; line with foil for easier cleanup
  • Exterior: Wipe stainless surfaces with stainless steel cleaner to maintain shine and remove fingerprints
  • Burners: Inspect annually; clean ports with wire brush if clogged

Seasonal Maintenance

  • Deep Clean: Perform thorough cleaning at season start and end
  • Burner Inspection: Check burner tubes for corrosion or damage (though stainless rarely has issues)
  • Ignition Check: Test all ignitions; replace batteries if needed
  • Gas Connections: Inspect hoses and connections for leaks using soapy water test

Winter Storage

  • Cover: Use quality grill cover year-round to protect from elements
  • Cold Climate: Grill can remain outside in winter; stainless construction is impervious to cold
  • Tank Storage: Disconnect and store propane tank per local regulations during extended non-use
