Tips for Success with Your Kamado Joe

Getting the most from your Classic III requires understanding some kamado-specific techniques:

Fuel Selection

  • Use Lump Charcoal: Kamado Joe recommends natural lump charcoal—never briquettes. Lump burns cleaner, hotter, and with better flavor.
  • Quality Matters: Premium lump charcoal like Kamado Joe Big Block or Jealous Devil produces less ash and burns more consistently.
  • Reusable Charcoal: Unburned lump can be reused for future cooks—just add fresh charcoal on top.

Temperature Management

  • Start Low: It's easier to raise temperature than lower it. Open vents gradually until you approach target temp.
  • Allow Stabilization: Give the ceramic 15-20 minutes to fully heat-soak and stabilize at temperature.
  • Small Adjustments: Tiny vent adjustments make big temperature changes. Move vents in small increments.
  • Be Patient: Temperature changes happen slowly on kamados—wait 10 minutes after vent adjustment before making further changes.

Maintenance

  • Burn Off Residue: After cooking, close vents to extinguish fire naturally. High heat self-cleans the interior.
  • Ash Removal: Clean ash drawer every 3-5 cooks for optimal airflow.
  • Gasket Replacement: Replace felt gasket every 1-2 years or when seal becomes loose.
  • Cover Protection: Use a quality cover to protect from weather when not in use.
  • Avoid Thermal Shock: Never pour water on hot ceramics—can cause cracking.
