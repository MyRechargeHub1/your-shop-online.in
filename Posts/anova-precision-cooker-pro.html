What Makes Sous Vide Special

The Finishing Step: Searing

Sous vide produces perfect doneness but doesn't create the browned, flavorful crust that makes steaks and chops appealing. That requires a finishing step.

Torch Finishing

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the extra power worth the premium over consumer models?

For frequent users or those cooking large batches, yes. The faster heating and better temperature recovery make cooking more convenient. For occasional users, less expensive models work fine.

Do I need special bags for sous vide?

Heavy-duty zip-lock bags work for most foods. For longer cooks or higher temperatures, dedicated vacuum-seal bags are recommended. A vacuum sealer isn't required but provides better results by removing all air from bags.

How long can I safely cook food?

Proper sous vide cooking is completely safe because the food is pasteurized at the cooking temperature. Beef can safely cook for 48+ hours. Chicken reaches pasteurization at 145Â°F in about 90 minutes. The app provides safe time guidelines for all foods.

Does it really taste better than traditionally cooked food?

For proteins especially, yes. The consistent doneness and retained moisture produce noticeably better results. The difference is particularly dramatic with chicken breast, pork chops, and inexpensive beef cuts.

Is it hard to learn?

Not at all. Set temperature, set time, drop food in bag into water. It's actually simpler than traditional cooking because you can't overcook by leaving food too long. The app's recipe library provides all the guidance needed.
