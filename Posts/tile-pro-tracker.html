The Tile Pro represents the company's flagship offering, designed for users who need maximum range, loudest alerts, and longest battery life. It's the tracker of choice for anyone serious about never losing their valuables again.

Voice Assistant Integration

Vehicle Location

Keep a Tile Pro in your car to mark its location in large parking lots. The 400-foot range covers most parking structures, and the app's map view shows exactly where you parked. This is particularly useful in massive mall parking lots or airport long-term parking.

Pet Tracking

While not a dedicated GPS pet tracker, Tile Pro can help locate nearby pets or notify you if your pet escapes the yard. Attach it to a collar using Tile's pet collar accessory. The water resistance handles outdoor conditions, though the community finding network is less reliable in rural areas.

Tool and Equipment Tracking

Contractors and DIY enthusiasts use Tile Pro to track expensive tools, preventing theft and making tools easy to locate in cluttered workshops or job sites. The durable construction withstands workshop environments, and the loud ring helps locate tools buried in toolboxes.

Customization Options

Comparison to Competitors

vs. Apple AirTag

AirTag benefits from Apple's massive Find My network (over 1 billion devices) and UWB precision finding, but only works with iPhones. Tile Pro offers platform flexibility, louder ring (119 dB vs. 60 dB), longer Bluetooth range (400 ft vs. 33 ft), and replaceable battery. AirTag wins for iPhone-only users; Tile Pro is better for everyone else.

vs. Samsung SmartTag

SmartTag and SmartTag+ work only with Samsung Galaxy devices, limiting usefulness compared to Tile's universal compatibility. SmartTag+ includes UWB like AirTag, but the ecosystem is much smaller. Tile Pro's louder ring and larger finding network make it more practical for most users.

vs. Chipolo

Chipolo ONE is Tile's closest direct competitor, offering similar features at comparable prices. Tile Pro edges ahead with longer range (400 ft vs. 200 ft), louder ring (119 dB vs. 120 dB is essentially equal), and larger finding network. Both are excellent choices; Tile's slightly broader ecosystem gives it a minor advantage.
