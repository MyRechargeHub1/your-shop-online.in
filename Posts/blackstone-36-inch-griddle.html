Seasoning and Maintenance

Like cast iron cookware, the Blackstone griddle top requires initial seasoning and regular maintenance to develop its non-stick surface. The process is straightforward but important for optimal performance and longevity.

Initial Seasoning Process

New griddles come with a protective coating that must be removed before first use. Clean the surface thoroughly with soapy water and a griddle scraper. After drying completely, apply a thin layer of high-smoke-point oil (canola, vegetable, or flaxseed oil work well) across the entire cooking surface. Heat the griddle until the oil begins to smoke, then allow it to cool. Repeat this process 3-4 times to build up the initial seasoning layer.

This seasoning creates a natural non-stick surface that improves with use. Foods release easily, cleanup becomes simpler, and the griddle develops rust protection. The dark patina that develops over time isn't dirtâ€”it's the polymerized oil layer that makes the griddle perform better.

After-Cooking Maintenance

Post-cooking cleanup is surprisingly simple. While the griddle is still warm (not hot), scrape off food debris with the included scraper. Pour a small amount of water on the surface (it will steam and bubble, loosening stuck-on food), then scrape again. Wipe clean with paper towels or a cloth. Finally, apply a thin layer of oil to protect the surface until next use. This entire process takes 5-10 minutes.

Avoid using soap on the seasoned surface, as it can strip the seasoning layer. For stuck-on messes, griddle stones or specialized griddle cleaners remove buildup without damaging the seasoning. With proper care, the cooking surface becomes increasingly non-stick and develops that beautiful black patina that signals a well-loved griddle.
