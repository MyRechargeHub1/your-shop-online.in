Massive 3840Wh LiFePO4 Battery Capacity

At the heart of the SOLIX F3800 is a high-capacity 3840Wh (3.84kWh) LiFePO4 (Lithium Iron Phosphate) battery, one of the largest capacities available in a portable power station. LiFePO4 chemistry offers significant advantages over standard lithium-ion batteries:

  • 3,000+ Cycle Lifespan: Retains 80% capacity after 3,000 charge cycles (approximately 10 years of daily use)
  • Superior Safety: More stable chemistry with lower risk of thermal runaway compared to NMC batteries
  • Wide Temperature Range: Operates efficiently in temperatures from -4Â°F to 104Â°F
  • Flat Discharge Curve: Maintains consistent voltage throughout discharge cycle for reliable device operation

This 3840Wh capacity translates to impressive real-world runtimes:

  • Full-size refrigerator: 40+ hours
  • Window A/C unit (1000W): 3+ hours
  • Electric kettle (1500W): 2+ hours
  • Laptop (50W): 60+ charges
  • Smartphone: 300+ charges
  • CPAP machine (30W): 100+ hours
  • Power tools (circular saw, drill): Multiple hours of active use

Expandable to 26.9kWh Capacity

One of the F3800's standout features is its modular expansion capability. You can connect up to six BP3800 expansion batteries (3.84kWh each) to reach a staggering 26.9kWh total capacity. This scalability makes the system adaptable to varying power needs:

  • Base Unit (3.84kWh): Weekend camping, emergency backup for essentials
  • + 1 Battery (7.68kWh): Extended off-grid living, multi-day power outages
  • + 2-3 Batteries (11.52-15.36kWh): Whole-home backup for 24+ hours, RV living
  • + 4-6 Batteries (19.2-26.9kWh): Multi-day home backup, professional worksite power

Expansion batteries connect via a simple cable system and are managed automatically by the F3800's intelligent battery management system (BMS), ensuring balanced charging and discharging across all units.

Ultra-Fast Charging Technology

Anker's GaNPrime charging technology enables remarkably fast recharge times:

  • AC Wall Charging: 0-80% in 1.5 hours (3000W max input via dual chargers)
  • Solar Charging: 2400W max solar input (0-100% in approximately 2 hours with optimal sun)
  • Car Charging: 12V/24V DC input for mobile charging on the road
  • Dual Charging: Combine AC + solar for up to 5400W input (0-80% in under 1 hour)

The ability to recharge from 0-80% in just 1.5 hours via AC power is groundbreaking for power stations in this capacity range. Most competitors require 4-8 hours for similar capacities. This fast charging is particularly valuable during rolling blackouts or when you need to quickly top up between power usage sessions.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can the F3800 be used as a UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)?

Yes, the F3800 features UPS functionality with a 20ms switchover time, which is fast enough for most electronics. However, for highly sensitive equipment like network servers or medical devices that require true zero-transfer-time UPS, a dedicated online UPS may still be preferable.

How many solar panels do I need to achieve 2400W solar input?

You'll need approximately 3000W of solar panel capacity to achieve 2400W real-world input (accounting for efficiency losses). This typically means 8-10 x 300W panels or 6-8 x 400W panels. Panels must be configured to stay within the 11-60V input range.

Can I use the F3800 while it's charging?Yes, the F3800 supports pass-through charging, allowing you to power devices while simultaneously recharging the battery. This is useful for maintaining continuous power during rolling blackouts or when you need to keep devices running while taking advantage of available charging time.

What's the difference between the F3800 and the F2000/F1500?

The F3800 is Anker's flagship model with 3840Wh capacity and 6000W output. The F2000 (2048Wh, 2000W) and F1500 (1536Wh, 1500W) are smaller, more portable options. The F3800 is the only model with expandable capacity to 26.9kWh and 240V output capability.

How long will the F3800 last before needing replacement?

With 3,000+ rated cycles to 80% capacity, and assuming daily full discharge/recharge, the battery would last approximately 8-10 years. Most users won't cycle it daily, so real-world lifespan of 10-15 years is reasonable. Anker's 5-year warranty covers defects and significant capacity degradation.
