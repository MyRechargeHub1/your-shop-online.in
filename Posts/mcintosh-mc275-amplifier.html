Introduction: A Legacy of Excellence

First introduced in 1961, the McIntosh MC275 revolutionized home audio with its innovative Unity Coupled Circuit output transformer design. This sixth-generation MC275 maintains the classic aesthetic and sonic signature while incorporating modern refinements that enhance reliability and performance. Handcrafted in Binghamton, New York, each amplifier represents the pinnacle of American audio engineering.

What distinguishes the MC275 from competitors is its unique ability to combine the warm, involving sound of tube amplification with the power and reliability typically associated with solid-state designs. The result is an amplifier that breathes life into music, revealing nuances and emotional content that lesser amplifiers cannot convey.

Legendary Tube Topology

The MC275's tube complement includes four 12AX7A and two 12AT7 tubes in the input and driver stages, with eight KT88 power tubes delivering 75 watts per channel. This push-pull design operates in triode mode, extracting the most musical performance from each tube while maintaining excellent linearity and low distortion.

Unity Coupled Circuit Technology

McIntosh's proprietary Unity Coupled Circuit output transformer design is the secret to the MC275's exceptional performance. This innovative topology ensures that each output tube operates in its optimal range regardless of speaker impedance or load characteristics. The result is consistent, musical sound with any loudspeakerâ€”from efficient horn systems to power-hungry planar magnetics.

The output transformers feature McIntosh's legendary build quality with hand-wound coils and premium materials. These transformers provide extremely wide bandwidth extending from 20Hz to well beyond 20kHz, ensuring the MC275 reproduces music with full tonal balance from the deepest bass notes to the airiest treble.
