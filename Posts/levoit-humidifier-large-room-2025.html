Understanding Humidity and Indoor Air Quality

Human comfort and health depend on humidity levels existing within specific ranges. Too-dry air (below 30% humidity) causes dry skin, chapped lips, static electricity buildup, and respiratory irritation. Overly humid air (above 60%) promotes mold growth and dust mites. The optimal range falls between 40-60% relative humidity, and most American homes drift toward the dry side, particularly during winter months.

Winter heating reduces humidity dramatically as dry outside air enters and gets warmedâ€”heated air's relative humidity drops substantially. Desert climates and heated buildings naturally develop humidity levels uncomfortable for occupants. Ultrasonic Humidification Technology

The LEVOIT uses ultrasonic technology rather than the evaporative approach of traditional humidifiers. A piezoelectric element vibrates at ultrasonic frequency, breaking water into microscopic particles that disperse as cool mist. This approach offers advantages: no heating required (cooler mist), whisper-quiet operation (no motor noise), and remarkable energy efficiency.

Traditional evaporative humidifiers work by blowing air through wet wicksâ€”noisier and less effective in very dry climates because dry air absorbs moisture slowly. The LEVOIT's ultrasonic approach delivers humidity rapidly and quietly, superior for serious dry conditions.
