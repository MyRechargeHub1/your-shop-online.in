M2 Chip: Desktop Performance in Tablet Form

The M2 chip in the iPad Pro delivers extraordinary performance that rivals many desktop computers. Built on second-generation 5-nanometer technology, the M2 features an 8-core CPU with four high-performance cores and four efficiency cores, providing up to 15% faster CPU performance than the previous M1 generation.

The 10-core GPU delivers up to 35% faster graphics performance, enabling professional 3D rendering, complex video editing, and demanding creative applications that were once impossible on tablets. The 16-core Neural Engine processes 15.8 trillion operations per second, accelerating machine learning tasks across iPadOS.

Real-World Performance Excellence

In practical use, the M2 iPad Pro handles tasks that would challenge many laptops. Edit multiple layers of 4K ProRes video in LumaFusion without dropped frames. Run complex Procreate illustrations with hundreds of layers without lag. Process RAW photos in Lightroom instantly. Export high-resolution renders in Nomad Sculpt faster than ever.

The unified memory architecture with up to 16GB RAM (on 1TB and 2TB models) ensures smooth multitasking. Run multiple demanding apps simultaneously, switch between them instantly, and maintain your workflow without performance degradation.

ProRes Video Acceleration

Dedicated ProRes encode and decode engines enable professional video workflows. Record ProRes video directly using compatible apps, edit multiple streams of ProRes footage, and export projects rapidly. For video professionals, this hardware acceleration makes the iPad Pro a legitimate editing workstation.

The media engine handles up to 8K H.264 and HEVC video, making it effortless to work with footage from professional cameras, drones, and smartphones. Color grading, effects, and transitions apply in real-time without rendering delays.

Stunning Liquid Retina XDR Display

The 12.9-inch Liquid Retina XDR display is simply spectacularâ€”one of the finest screens ever installed in any computing device. With 2732 x 2048 resolution at 264 pixels per inch, text appears razor-sharp and images display with extraordinary clarity.

Mini-LED Excellence

Over 10,000 mini-LEDs organized into 2,596 local dimming zones create extraordinary contrast and HDR performance. Peak brightness reaches 1,600 nits for HDR content and 1,000 nits for standard content, ensuring excellent visibility even in bright sunlight.

The 1,000,000:1 contrast ratio produces genuinely deep blacks alongside brilliant highlights. Watching HDR movies reveals details in shadows that are invisible on most displays, while highlights shine without blooming into adjacent areas. The local dimming is so precise that halos and blooming are virtually imperceptible.

ProMotion and True Tone

ProMotion technology delivers adaptive refresh rates up to 120Hz, creating incredibly smooth scrolling, responsive touch interactions, and natural motion. The display intelligently adjusts from 24Hz to 120Hz based on content, balancing smoothness with battery efficiency.

True Tone adjusts the display's color temperature based on ambient lighting, ensuring colors appear natural in any environment. The wide P3 color gamut provides vibrant, accurate colors that creative professionals can trust for color-critical work.

Reference Mode for Professionals

Reference Mode matches color requirements for professional workflows, supporting industry-standard color spaces including sRGB, Display P3, and specific broadcast standards. Photographers, videographers, and designers can trust that colors viewed on the iPad Pro accurately represent how they'll appear in final output.

Apple Pencil Hover: A Game-Changing Innovation

Apple Pencil (2nd generation) hover detection represents a fundamental advancement in digital drawing and note-taking. The iPad Pro senses the Apple Pencil up to 12mm above the screen, showing a preview of your mark before you commit to it.

Enhanced Creative Workflows

For artists, hover preview transforms the creative process. See exactly where your brush stroke will appear, preview pen width and opacity, and position elements precisely before touching the screen. This seemingly simple feature dramatically improves precision and speeds up illustration workflows.

In note-taking apps like GoodNotes and Notability, hover highlights text boxes before you write in them, shows handwriting recognition previews, and makes interface interaction more precise. The experience feels natural, similar to working with traditional pen and paper.
