Samsung Galaxy Tab A9+: Exceptional Value in Android Tablets

November 24, 2024 10 min read Tablets

The Samsung Galaxy Tab A9+ delivers impressive features at a budget-friendly price point, making premium tablet experiences accessible to a wider audience. With an 11-inch display, quad speakers, and expandable storage, it's the ideal choice for families, students, and anyone seeking an affordable Android tablet.

Democratizing Tablet Technology

Not everyone needs or can afford flagship tablet technology. Samsung recognizes this with their Galaxy Tab A series, which brings essential tablet features to price-conscious consumers without major compromises. The Tab A9+ (the "plus" indicating the larger 11-inch model) specifically targets users who want a sizeable screen for media consumption, light productivity, and family use without the premium price tag of the Tab S series.

The Tab A9+ succeeds by focusing on what matters most: a large, quality display, all-day battery life, decent performance for typical tasks, and thoughtful features like expandable storage and a 3.5mm headphone jack that flagship tablets often omit.

Design and Build Quality

The Galaxy Tab A9+ features a metal body that gives it a more premium feel than its price suggests. The predominantly graphite or silver finish looks sophisticated, and the slim 6.9mm profile makes it surprisingly portable for an 11-inch tablet. At approximately 1.1 pounds (480 grams), it's light enough for comfortable one-handed use during short periods.

Practical Design Choices

Samsung has made several practical design decisions that enhance daily usability. The bezels surrounding the display are moderately sized - not as thin as premium tablets but slim enough to look modern while providing practical grip area to avoid accidental touch inputs.

The power button and volume rocker are positioned on the right side (when held vertically), falling naturally under your fingers. The USB-C port on the bottom handles charging and data transfer. Notably, the Tab A9+ retains a 3.5mm headphone jack - increasingly rare on tablets but valuable for users with wired headphones or who want to avoid Bluetooth latency.

Display: The Star of the Show

The 11-inch TFT LCD display is the Tab A9+'s centerpiece. With a resolution of 1920 x 1200 pixels (WUXGA), it delivers sharp, detailed visuals suitable for everything from streaming video to reading documents. The 16:10 aspect ratio works well for both landscape media viewing and portrait reading or browsing.

Visual Performance

While not matching AMOLED's perfect blacks and infinite contrast, the LCD panel delivers good color reproduction, adequate brightness for indoor use, and respectable viewing angles. Colors appear natural rather than oversaturated, which some users prefer for extended viewing sessions.

The 90Hz refresh rate (model dependent - some variants are 60Hz) provides noticeably smoother scrolling and animations compared to standard 60Hz displays. This may seem like a minor spec, but the improved fluidity genuinely enhances the everyday user experience.

For media consumption - streaming Netflix, Disney+, YouTube - the display performs admirably. The large 11-inch size creates an immersive viewing experience, and the resolution is sharp enough that individual pixels aren't visible at normal viewing distances.

Performance and Hardware

The Tab A9+ uses a mid-range processor (typically Qualcomm Snapdragon 695 5G or similar) paired with 4GB or 8GB of RAM depending on configuration. This isn't flagship power, but it's appropriate for the tablet's target use cases.

Real-World Performance

For typical tablet tasks, the Tab A9+ performs smoothly:

  • Web Browsing: Multiple browser tabs load and switch without lag
  • Media Streaming: Netflix, YouTube, and other services play without stuttering
  • Social Media: Apps like Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok run smoothly
  • Light Gaming: Casual games run well; demanding 3D games may require reduced settings
  • Document Editing: Google Docs, Microsoft Office apps handle typical productivity tasks
  • Multitasking: Split-screen mode works adequately with mainstream apps

Where you'll notice the mid-range processor is in heavy gaming, professional video editing, or running many demanding apps simultaneously. For users who primarily consume media and handle basic productivity, the performance is perfectly adequate.

Storage and Expandability

The Tab A9+ typically comes with 64GB or 128GB of internal storage. While the base 64GB model fills up quickly with apps and media, Samsung includes a microSD card slot supporting cards up to 1TB. This expandability is a significant advantage over tablets like iPads that lack expandable storage.

Storage Recommendations

If you plan to store movies, music, or photos locally, invest in a microSD card. High-capacity cards (128GB-256GB) are affordable and effectively unlimited storage for typical users. You can move media files and some apps to the SD card, keeping internal storage free for system operations and frequently-used apps.

Audio Quality

One of the Tab A9+'s standout features is its quad speaker system. Four speakers positioned on the tablet's edges create surprisingly immersive stereo sound for a budget tablet. While lacking the bass depth of premium tablets with larger speakers, the audio is clear, well-balanced, and gets adequately loud for personal use and small group viewing.

The speakers support Dolby Atmos, which processes audio for enhanced spatial sound. While your experience of Atmos depends on the source content, it generally improves the soundstage and creates a more enveloping audio experience for movies and music.

The included 3.5mm headphone jack is valuable for private listening, wired headphones with no latency (important for video watching), and connecting to external speakers without Bluetooth complications.

Battery Life: All-Day Reliability

The Tab A9+ includes a generous 7,040mAh battery that provides excellent endurance. Samsung rates it at approximately 12 hours of video playback, and real-world use confirms strong battery performance. Typical mixed use - web browsing, video streaming, some gaming - easily delivers 8-10 hours of screen-on time.

For families sharing a tablet or students using it throughout school days, this battery capacity means the tablet rarely needs mid-day charging. Weekend trips rarely require bringing the charger along. This reliability is one of the Tab A9+'s most practical advantages.

Charging

The tablet charges via USB-C with the included charger. Fast charging support (typically 15W) provides faster recharging than older slow chargers, though it's not as rapid as premium tablets with 25W+ charging. A full charge takes approximately 3-4 hours from empty.

Software and Android Experience

The Galaxy Tab A9+ runs Android with Samsung's One UI overlay. One UI is generally well-regarded for its clean design, useful features, and regular updates. Samsung has committed to providing several years of software updates for the Tab A series, ensuring security and feature improvements.

One UI Features

  • Multi-Window: Run two apps side-by-side for multitasking
  • Pop-Up View: Run apps in floating windows for quick access
  • Edge Panels: Swipe-in panels provide quick access to apps and tools
  • Samsung Kids: Kid-friendly mode with parental controls and educational content
  • Samsung DeX: Desktop-like interface when connected to external displays (feature availability varies by model)

App Ecosystem

As an Android tablet, the Tab A9+ accesses the Google Play Store's extensive app library. While Android tablet app optimization has historically lagged behind iPadOS, major apps like Netflix, YouTube, Spotify, Microsoft Office, and Google's suite are well-optimized for tablet displays.

For creative work, apps like Adobe Lightroom Mobile, Krita, and various note-taking apps work well. The experience won't match iPad with Apple Pencil for professional creative work, but for casual and intermediate users, Android offers plenty of capable apps.

Camera System

The Tab A9+ includes an 8MP rear camera and a 5MP front camera. These are functional rather than impressive - adequate for video calls, document scanning, and casual photos but not for serious photography. Tablet cameras generally lag behind smartphones since few users rely on tablets as their primary cameras.

The front camera handles video calls well enough for Zoom, Google Meet, and FaceTime (via web browser). Video quality is acceptable with good lighting but degrades in dim conditions. For the tablet's target use cases - occasional video calls and document scanning - the cameras are sufficient.

Use Cases: Who Should Buy This Tablet?

Families

The Tab A9+ is excellent as a shared family tablet. The large screen works well for group viewing, Samsung Kids mode keeps younger users safe, and the affordable price means less anxiety about kids using it. The durability and long battery life suit family use scenarios.

Students

For students needing a device for note-taking, research, document creation, and media consumption, the Tab A9+ provides essential functionality at a budget-friendly price. It handles typical schoolwork, online learning platforms, and educational apps without issue.

Media Consumers

If your primary tablet use is streaming video, browsing the web, reading, and social media, the Tab A9+ excels. The large display and good audio create an excellent media consumption experience rivaling much more expensive tablets.

Casual Gamers

While not a gaming powerhouse, the Tab A9+ handles casual gaming well. Mobile games like Among Us, Candy Crush, Stardew Valley, and most popular titles run smoothly. The large display makes gaming more immersive than phone screens.

Budget-Conscious Buyers

Anyone wanting tablet functionality without premium pricing will appreciate the Tab A9+. It delivers essential features - large screen, good battery life, expandable storage - without the compromises that often plague ultra-cheap tablets.

Accessories

Samsung and third-party manufacturers offer various accessories for the Tab A9+:

  • Book Cover Cases: Provide protection and fold into stands for hands-free viewing
  • Screen Protectors: Protect the display from scratches
  • Bluetooth Keyboards: Transform the tablet for productivity work
  • MicroSD Cards: Expand storage affordably
  • USB-C Hubs: Add HDMI, USB-A, and other connectivity

While Samsung doesn't make a premium keyboard case like iPad's Magic Keyboard for the Tab A9+, numerous affordable third-party keyboard cases are available that work well for occasional typing.

Limitations to Consider

While the Tab A9+ offers excellent value, it has limitations compared to premium tablets:

  • The LCD display doesn't match OLED's contrast and color vibrancy
  • Performance lags behind flagship tablets for demanding tasks
  • Android tablet app optimization still trails iPadOS in some categories
  • Build quality, while decent, isn't as premium as higher-priced models
  • No S Pen stylus support (available on higher-end Samsung tablets)
  • Cameras are adequate but not impressive

These trade-offs are reasonable given the price point. The Tab A9+ delivers what matters most - large display, good battery life, adequate performance - while economizing on less essential features.

Comparison to Competitors

Vs. iPad 10th Generation

Apple's base iPad offers better performance, superior app ecosystem, and longer software support. However, it costs significantly more, lacks expandable storage, and doesn't include a headphone jack. The Tab A9+ provides better value for budget-conscious buyers, especially Android users.

Vs. Lenovo Tab M11

Lenovo's competing Android tablet offers similar specs at comparable pricing. The choice often comes down to brand preference and specific feature differences. Samsung generally provides better software support and ecosystem integration for Samsung device owners.

Vs. Amazon Fire HD 11

Amazon's Fire tablets are cheaper but run Fire OS (limited version of Android) with restricted app selection through Amazon's app store rather than Google Play. The Tab A9+ offers full Android flexibility and better performance.

Getting the Most from Your Tab A9+

  • Invest in a large microSD card (128-256GB) to avoid storage concerns
  • Use Samsung Kids mode if sharing with children
  • Explore split-screen multitasking for productivity
  • Consider a book cover case for protection and stand functionality
  • Download content for offline viewing to maximize battery life
  • Adjust display settings to your preferences (color mode, brightness)
  • Set up family accounts for personalized experiences
  • Use edge panels for quick access to favorite apps and features

Final Verdict

The Samsung Galaxy Tab A9+ is exactly what a budget tablet should be: it delivers core tablet experiences exceptionally well while making smart compromises to hit an affordable price point. The large, quality display creates an immersive viewing experience. The quad speakers provide surprisingly good audio. The generous battery ensures all-day use. The expandable storage prevents capacity anxiety.

This isn't a tablet for professional video editors, serious mobile gamers, or users demanding cutting-edge performance. It's a tablet for everyone else - families watching movies together, students taking notes and researching, casual gamers playing mobile hits, readers enjoying digital books, and anyone who wants a large-screen device for web browsing and apps without spending flagship prices.

The Tab A9+ succeeds by understanding its audience and delivering precisely what they need. Samsung hasn't tried to create a budget version of their premium Tab S tablets. Instead, they've crafted a purpose-built device that excels at common tablet tasks while remaining affordable.

For Android users, families, students, or anyone seeking their first tablet, the Galaxy Tab A9+ represents exceptional value. It's proof that you don't need to spend premium prices to get a genuinely useful, enjoyable tablet experience. If your tablet needs match what the Tab A9+ offers - and for most people, they do - it's an easy recommendation that won't disappoint.
