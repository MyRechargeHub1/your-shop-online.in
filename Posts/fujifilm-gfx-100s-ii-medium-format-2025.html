Introduction: Medium Format for Everyone

The Fujifilm GFX 100S II represents a remarkable achievement in camera design: it brings the stunning image quality of a 102-megapixel medium format sensor into a body that's barely larger than a professional full-frame camera. Priced at around $5,000, it makes medium format photography accessible to a much wider audience than ever before, offering image quality that rivals cameras costing twice as much. This isn't just a camera for studio photographers anymore—it's a versatile tool that can handle everything from landscape photography to street shooting, from portraits to commercial work.

In this comprehensive review, we'll explore every aspect of the GFX 100S II, from its breathtaking 102-megapixel sensor and advanced autofocus system to its surprisingly compact form factor and professional features. Whether you're a landscape photographer seeking maximum detail, a portrait photographer wanting that signature medium format look, or a commercial shooter needing files that can be printed at any size, the GFX 100S II delivers performance that will transform your photography. This is medium format reimagined for the modern photographer.

Revolutionary 102-Megapixel Medium Format Sensor

At the heart of the GFX 100S II is one of the most impressive image sensors ever created:

Large Format Sensor

  • 43.8 x 32.9mm Sensor: The sensor is 1.7 times larger than full-frame, providing exceptional image quality and shallow depth of field.
  • 102 Megapixels: Captures an incredible 11,648 x 8,736 pixels—enough resolution for billboard-sized prints.
  • Medium Format Advantage: Larger pixels mean better light gathering, improved dynamic range, and superior color depth.
  • 16-Bit RAW: Captures 16-bit RAW files with extraordinary tonal gradation and editing flexibility.
  • 15 Stops Dynamic Range: Preserves detail in both highlights and shadows like no full-frame camera can.

Image Quality

The image quality from the GFX 100S II is simply breathtaking:

  • Incredible Detail: The 102MP resolution captures an almost three-dimensional level of detail that must be seen to be believed.
  • Signature Medium Format Look: That special quality that only medium format can deliver—rich tonality, smooth gradations, and beautiful depth.
  • Outstanding Color Science: Fujifilm's legendary color science delivers accurate, pleasing colors straight out of camera.
  • Film Simulations: Includes Fujifilm's famous film simulation modes that replicate classic film stocks.
  • Low-Light Performance: Native ISO range of 80-12,800, expandable to 102,400 with remarkably clean files.

X-Processor 5

  • Latest-generation processor handles the massive 102MP files effortlessly
  • Faster processing speeds improve overall camera responsiveness
  • Advanced AI algorithms power improved autofocus and subject detection
  • Enables high-resolution video recording capabilities

Advanced Autofocus System

The GFX 100S II features a dramatically improved autofocus system compared to its predecessor:

425-Point Phase Detection AF

  • Full Sensor Coverage: AF points cover approximately 100% of the frame for flexible composition.
  • Phase Detection: Fast, accurate phase-detection autofocus throughout the focus range.
  • Low-Light Capability: Focuses down to -7 EV—that's darker than the human eye can see.
  • Face/Eye Detection: Automatically detects and tracks faces and eyes for perfect portrait focus.
  • Subject Detection AF: Recognizes and tracks various subjects including humans, animals, birds, cars, motorcycles, bicycles, airplanes, and trains.

Continuous Shooting

  • 7fps Continuous: Shoots up to 7 frames per second with full AF tracking.
  • Pre-Shot Mode: Captures images before you fully press the shutter for never missing critical moments.
  • Large Buffer: Shoot dozens of 102MP RAW files before the buffer fills.
  • Electronic Shutter: Silent electronic shutter option for discrete shooting.

Surprisingly Compact and Portable

One of the GFX 100S II's greatest strengths is its remarkably compact size for a medium format camera:

Compact Body Design

  • Lightweight Construction: Weighs only 900g (body only)—lighter than many professional full-frame cameras.
  • Compact Dimensions: Measures just 5.9 x 4.1 x 3.4 inches—barely larger than full-frame mirrorless cameras.
  • Magnesium Alloy Body: Durable yet lightweight magnesium alloy construction.
  • Weather Sealing: Sealed against dust and moisture at 79 points for reliable outdoor shooting.
  • Cold-Weather Operation: Functions in temperatures down to 14°F (-10°C).

Ergonomics and Handling

  • Deep Grip: Comfortable grip provides secure handling even with larger lenses.
  • Intuitive Controls: Well-placed buttons and dials for efficient operation.
  • Top LCD Panel: Convenient top display shows key shooting settings at a glance.
  • Dual Command Dials: Front and rear dials provide quick access to key settings.
  • Customizable Buttons: Numerous buttons can be programmed for your preferred functions.

Professional Viewfinder and Display

  • 9.44 Million Dot EVF: Industry-leading ultra-high-resolution electronic viewfinder.
  • 0.84x Magnification: Large, bright view with excellent eye relief.
  • 120fps Refresh Rate: Smooth, lag-free viewing experience.
  • Tilting Touchscreen: 3.2-inch vari-angle touchscreen LCD for flexible shooting angles.
  • 1.62 Million Dots: High-resolution LCD screen clearly shows 102MP images.

Professional Video Capabilities

The GFX 100S II isn't just for still photography—it's also a capable video camera:

4K Video Recording

  • 4K at 60p: Records 4K (3840 x 2160) video at up to 60 frames per second.
  • Full-Width 4K: Uses the entire width of the medium format sensor for cinematic shallow depth of field.
  • 10-Bit 4:2:2 Internal: Records 10-bit color internally for professional color grading.
  • F-Log2: Flat log profile preserves maximum dynamic range for post-production.
  • ProRes Support: Can output Apple ProRes formats for professional workflows.

Video Features

  • Subject detection AF works in video mode
  • Face/eye tracking maintains focus on subjects
  • Focus peaking and zebras for exposure monitoring
  • Timecode support for multi-camera productions
  • External recording via HDMI output
  • Microphone and headphone jacks for audio monitoring

In-Body Image Stabilization

The GFX 100S II features highly effective in-body stabilization:

  • Up to 8 Stops Compensation: Industry-leading stabilization compensates for up to 8 stops of camera shake.
  • 5-Axis Stabilization: Corrects for pitch, yaw, roll, horizontal shift, and vertical shift.
  • Works with All Lenses: Provides stabilization even with non-stabilized lenses.
  • Pixel Shift Multi-Shot: Special mode captures multiple shifted images for even higher resolution.
  • Video Stabilization: IBIS works during video recording for smooth handheld footage.

GF Lens System

The GFX 100S II is part of Fujifilm's outstanding GF medium format lens system:

GF Lens Excellence

  • Exceptional Optical Quality: GF lenses are designed specifically to resolve the 102MP sensor.
  • Extensive Selection: Over 20 native GF lenses covering all focal lengths.
  • Professional Build: Weather-sealed construction with metal barrels and smooth operation.
  • Fast Apertures: Many lenses offer bright f/2.8 or faster apertures for creative control.
  • Compact Design: GF lenses are surprisingly compact for medium format optics.

Recommended Lenses

  • GF 50mm f/3.5: Compact, affordable standard lens perfect for everyday shooting.
  • GF 32-64mm f/4: Versatile standard zoom covering wide to short telephoto.
  • GF 80mm f/1.7: Stunning portrait lens with beautiful bokeh.
  • GF 20-35mm f/4: Ultra-wide zoom ideal for landscapes and architecture.
  • GF 110mm f/2: Professional portrait lens with exceptional rendering.

Connectivity and Workflow

The GFX 100S II offers comprehensive connectivity for professional workflows:

Wireless Connectivity

  • Built-in Wi-Fi: 5GHz and 2.4GHz Wi-Fi for fast wireless transfer.
  • Bluetooth: Constant low-energy connection to smartphones.
  • Fujifilm Camera Remote App: Free iOS and Android app for wireless control and image transfer.
  • Wireless Tethering: Shoot tethered wirelessly to computer or mobile device.

Wired Connections

  • USB-C 3.2: High-speed USB-C for fast file transfer and tethering.
  • HDMI Output: Micro HDMI port for external recording and monitoring.
  • Microphone Input: 3.5mm microphone jack for external audio.
  • Headphone Output: 3.5mm headphone jack for audio monitoring.
  • Flash Sync: PC sync terminal for studio flash systems.

Memory Cards

  • Dual SD card slots (UHS-II compatible)
  • Overflow, backup, or RAW+JPEG separation modes
  • Fast UHS-II cards recommended for video and continuous shooting

Battery Life and Power

  • NP-W235 Battery: High-capacity battery provides approximately 540 shots per charge.
  • USB-C Charging: Charge battery in-camera via USB-C port.
  • USB Power Delivery: Operate camera indefinitely on external USB-C power.
  • Power Saving Mode: Economy mode extends battery life for longer shooting sessions.
  • Battery Percentage: Precise percentage display shows remaining charge.

Price and Value

The Fujifilm GFX 100S II is priced at approximately $5,000, making it one of the most accessible medium format cameras ever created. While this is certainly a significant investment, the value proposition is extraordinary when you consider what you're getting: a 102-megapixel medium format sensor delivering image quality that rivals cameras costing $8,000 or more, in a body that's compact enough for practical everyday use.

Competitive Context: Traditional medium format cameras like the Hasselblad X2D 100C cost $8,000 (body only), while the Phase One XF IQ4 150MP system costs well over $50,000. The GFX 100S II delivers comparable image quality at a fraction of the price. Even compared to high-resolution full-frame cameras, the medium format sensor provides advantages in dynamic range, color depth, and that intangible medium format look that can't be replicated.

Total System Cost: While the camera body is relatively affordable, remember that GF lenses range from $1,000 to $4,000+ each. A complete professional system with 2-3 lenses could total $10,000-$15,000. However, the investment pays dividends in image quality that opens up professional opportunities and delivers files that clients will pay premium rates for.

Long-Term Value: Medium format cameras tend to hold their value extremely well. Photographers who bought the original GFX 100S can still sell it for 50-60% of its original price years later. The GFX 100S II will likely remain relevant and valuable for 5-7 years or more, making the per-year cost very reasonable for working professionals.

Conclusion: Medium Format Perfected

The Fujifilm GFX 100S II represents the culmination of Fujifilm's vision to make medium format photography accessible to a wider audience. By packing a 102-megapixel sensor into a body barely larger than full-frame cameras, and pricing it at $5,000, Fujifilm has created a camera that changes the equation for photographers who demand ultimate image quality. This isn't a specialized studio camera—it's a versatile tool that can handle landscape photography, portraiture, commercial work, weddings, and even street photography.

The image quality is simply stunning. The 102 megapixels capture an incredible level of detail that reveals textures and nuances invisible to full-frame sensors. The 15-stop dynamic range preserves detail in both highlights and shadows that other cameras clip. The medium format sensor delivers that special quality—rich colors, smooth tonal gradations, and beautiful depth—that makes images almost three-dimensional. Combined with Fujifilm's legendary color science and film simulations, the GFX 100S II produces files that are gorgeous straight out of camera yet offer incredible latitude for post-processing.

The compact size is a revelation. At just 900g, the GFX 100S II is lighter than many professional full-frame cameras with battery grips. You can handhold it all day without fatigue. You can pack it in a regular camera bag with a few lenses. It doesn't draw attention on the street. This changes what's possible with medium format—it's no longer limited to tripod-based landscape and studio work. The weather sealing and robust build inspire confidence in any environment.

Yes, it's more expensive than full-frame cameras. Yes, the lenses cost more. Yes, the files are massive and require substantial storage. But for photographers who see the difference that ultimate image quality makes—who need files that can be printed at any size, who want that signature medium format look, who demand professional-grade dynamic range and color—the Fujifilm GFX 100S II delivers value that far exceeds its price. This is medium format for the real world, and it's absolutely brilliant.
