Frequently Asked Questions

Are the Sony WH-1000XM5 worth upgrading from the XM4?

If you prioritize comfort and have the budget, yes. The XM5 offers better noise cancellation, improved comfort, and refined sound. However, the XM4 remains excellent and costs less. If you're happy with your XM4s, there's no urgent need to upgrade.

Can I use the XM5 with a cable when the battery dies?

Unfortunately, no. Unlike the XM4, the XM5 cannot operate in wired mode when the battery is depleted. However, with 30-hour battery life and quick charging, this rarely becomes an issue.

Do these work well for gaming?

Yes, though Bluetooth latency exists for competitive gaming. The XM5 works great for single-player games and casual gaming. For competitive multiplayer, consider wired gaming headphones for zero latency.

How is the noise cancellation compared to Bose?

The Sony XM5 and Bose QuietComfort Ultra trade blows for best ANC. Sony edges ahead slightly in blocking constant drone (airplanes), while Bose excels at sudden sounds (voices). Both are excellent choices.

Are these good for working out?

While possible, the XM5 isn't designed for exercise. The over-ear design can get warm during workouts, and the lack of water resistance means sweat could cause damage. Consider dedicated sport earbuds like Sony WF-1000XM5 for exercise.
