Beyond Emotional Reactivity

Singer teaches that most workplace dysfunction stems not from external circumstances but from our emotional reactions to those circumstances. Two professionals facing identical challenges experience vastly different outcomes based on how emotionally reactive they become. When someone challenges your idea, you can become defensive and dismissive, or you can listen to understand their perspective. When a project fails, you can blame circumstances or team members, or you can examine what you might have done differently. Singer's framework involves observing your emotional reactions rather than being controlled by them, creating space for conscious choice regarding how to respond.
