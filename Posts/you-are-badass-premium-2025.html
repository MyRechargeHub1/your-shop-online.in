Identifying Your Limiting Beliefs

The first step toward change is identifying the specific beliefs sabotaging your life. Sincero guides readers in recognizing limiting beliefs by paying attention to their patterns. Do you chronically undercharge for your work? That probably stems from a belief that you're not worth more. Do you self-sabotage when you're close to success? That probably reflects a belief that success isn't for people like you. Do you stay in situations that make you unhappy? That probably involves a belief that you don't deserve better or can't handle change. These beliefs operate largely unconsciously, yet they direct your choices and shape your reality.

Sincero emphasizes that identifying these beliefs isn't about judgment or self-blame. It's about seeing the system clearly so you can change it. The Neuroscience of Belief

While Sincero developed her philosophy through personal experience and coaching, contemporary neuroscience validates her insights completely. The brain is a prediction machine; it unconsciously notices patterns and creates expectations about future events. These expectations literally influence perceptionâ€”you notice information confirming your expectations and filter out contradicting information. If you believe "I'm not creative," your brain unconsciously filters out evidence of your creativity. Over time, your brain updates its predictions, and what once seemed impossible begins to feel inevitable.
