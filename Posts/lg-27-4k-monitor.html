Professional Color Accuracy

Color accuracy separates professional monitors from consumer displays, and the LG 27-inch 4K UHD delivers in this critical area. The monitor covers 99% of the sRGB color space with factory calibration that ensures accurate color reproduction right out of the box.

Factory Calibration

Each monitor undergoes individual factory calibration, with color accuracy typically achieving Delta E < 2 â€“ the threshold below which color differences are imperceptible to the human eye. USB-C Connectivity with Power Delivery

Perhaps one of the most practical features for modern professionals is the USB-C connectivity with 60W Power Delivery. This single-cable solution simultaneously transmits video, audio, and power, dramatically simplifying cable management and enabling seamless laptop connectivity.

Single-Cable Solution

Built-in USB Hub

Setup and Optimization Tips

Initial Calibration

While the factory calibration is excellent, take time to adjust brightness to your ambient lighting conditions. Most users find 200-250 nits comfortable for office environments, while darker rooms may prefer 150-200 nits. The monitor defaults to maximum brightness, which is usually too bright and can cause eye strain.

USB-C Configuration

For optimal USB-C performance, ensure your laptop supports DisplayPort Alt Mode over USB-C and can deliver sufficient display signal. Most modern laptops do, but older USB-C ports may only support data without video. Check your laptop specifications if the USB-C connection doesn't work as expected.

Text Scaling on Windows

Windows users should configure display scaling appropriately for the 4K resolution. The default 150% scaling provides comfortable text size for most users, though you can adjust between 100-200% based on your preferences and vision. macOS handles scaling automatically and generally requires no adjustment.
