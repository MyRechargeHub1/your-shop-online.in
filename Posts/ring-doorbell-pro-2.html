Game-Changing 3D Motion Detection

Perhaps the most innovative feature of the Ring Video Doorbell Pro 2 is its radar-powered 3D motion detection system. Unlike traditional motion sensors that simply detect movement, the Pro 2's advanced radar technology tracks objects in three-dimensional space, determining their size, distance, and trajectory.

How 3D Motion Detection Works

The integrated radar sensor continuously monitors the space in front of your door, creating a three-dimensional map up to 30 feet away. When movement is detected, the system doesn't just trigger an alert â€“ it analyzes the object's path, speed, and direction to determine if it represents a legitimate concern.

This intelligence dramatically reduces false alerts. A car driving past on the street won't trigger notifications, but a person walking up your driveway will. The system can distinguish between a delivery person approaching your door and simply passing by on the sidewalk. This contextual awareness makes the Pro 2 significantly smarter than competitors.

Customizable Motion Zones

The Ring app allows you to create up to three custom motion zones, defining exactly which areas you want to monitor. Draw these zones precisely on the live view to exclude high-traffic sidewalks while ensuring your driveway and porch are fully covered. This granular control ensures you're alerted to meaningful events while ignoring irrelevant movement.

Innovative Bird's Eye View Technology

Exclusive to the Pro 2, Bird's Eye View creates an overhead aerial map showing the path visitors take as they approach your door. Using the radar data, the system generates a top-down visualization that shows exactly where someone walked, how long they remained at your door, and which direction they departed.

This feature is particularly valuable for security reviews. If a package goes missing, you can see whether the delivery person placed it at your door or elsewhere on your property. If someone suspicious is casing homes in your neighborhood, you'll have a clear record of their approach pattern and departure route.

The Bird's Eye View appears automatically in the Ring app after a motion event, requiring no additional configuration. It's an elegant solution that transforms abstract motion data into instantly understandable visual information.

Pre-Roll Video Preview

Pre-Roll is another standout feature that captures 4-6 seconds of black-and-white footage before a motion event triggers. This provides crucial context about what happened immediately before someone rang your doorbell or triggered the motion sensor.

In practice, Pre-Roll means you'll see how a delivery person approached your door, whether visitors knocked before ringing the bell, or what caused an unexpected alert. This context has proven invaluable for many Ring users, often capturing the most important moments that would otherwise be missed.

Note that Pre-Roll requires a Ring Protect subscription and relies on the doorbell's wired power connection to maintain the constant recording buffer.

Installation and Setup

The Ring Video Doorbell Pro 2 requires hardwired installation, connecting to your existing doorbell wiring. This provides continuous power, enabling features like Pre-Roll and 24/7 connectivity without battery concerns.

Installation Requirements

Your home's doorbell transformer must provide 16-24 VAC (50/60 Hz) at 10-40 volt-amps â€“ higher than many older doorbells require. Ring includes a Power Kit Pro, installed at your transformer or chime, that ensures proper voltage delivery and protects your traditional chime from damage.

The installation process takes 30-60 minutes for someone comfortable with basic electrical work. Ring provides detailed video instructions, and the app guides you through each step. However, if you're uncomfortable working with household wiring, professional installation is recommended and available through Ring for an additional fee.

Initial Configuration

Setup through the Ring app is straightforward. The app walks you through connecting the doorbell to your WiFi network (2.4GHz or 5GHz supported), configuring motion detection zones, setting up notification preferences, and testing the doorbell functionality.

The Pro 2 includes dual-band WiFi support, allowing connection to both 2.4GHz (better range) and 5GHz (better speed) networks. The app includes a WiFi signal strength test, helping you verify adequate connectivity before completing installation.

Ring Protect Subscription Plans

While the Ring Video Doorbell Pro 2 functions without a subscription, a Ring Protect plan is necessary to unlock video recording, review past events, and enable features like Pre-Roll and Rich Notifications with video previews.

Ring Protect Basic

At $4.99/month or $49.99/year, Ring Protect Basic covers a single device with 180 days of video history, photo capture on motion events, and sharing and saving of videos. This is the minimum subscription recommended to get real value from your Ring doorbell.

Ring Protect Plus

For $10/month or $100/year, Ring Protect Plus covers all Ring devices at one location, adds extended warranties, and includes 10% off Ring products. If you have multiple Ring cameras or plan to expand your Ring security system, Plus provides better value.

Without Subscription

Without a subscription, you can still receive real-time notifications, view live video, and use two-way audio. However, you cannot review or save recordings, which significantly limits the doorbell's security value. Past events are simply not accessible without a subscription.
