Comparison to Water Rowers and Magnetic Rowers

Potential buyers often wonder how the RowErg's air resistance compares to water resistance and magnetic resistance alternatives. Each system has characteristics worth understanding.

Air Resistance (RowErg)

Provides the most accurate simulation of actual water rowing. Resistance increases proportionally with your effortâ€”pull harder, feel more resistance automatically. The flywheel inertia creates a natural catch and drive feel. Air rowers tend to be the most durable and require minimal maintenance.

Water Resistance

Creates a soothing water swooshing sound and some users prefer the visual and auditory aesthetics. However, water resistance can feel less consistent as water temperature and fill level affect resistance. Water rowers also require periodic water treatment to prevent algae and may need occasional refilling.

Magnetic Resistance

Operates very quietly and provides smooth resistance. However, magnetic systems don't automatically scale resistance to effortâ€”you must manually adjust resistance levels. They also tend to cost more and have more complex electronics that may need eventual service.

For pure performance, durability, and rowing feel, air resistance remains the choice of competitive rowers and serious athletes. The RowErg's air system delivers unmatched reliability and authentic rowing simulation.
