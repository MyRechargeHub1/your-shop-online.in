Safety Considerations and Responsible Use

Supervision Requirements

Despite the safety net and padding, adult supervision remains essential, particularly for younger children. Trampolines carry inherent fall and impact risks. Adult presence prevents dangerous behaviors like somersaults (which increase injury risk) and ensures children use the trampoline safely and appropriately.

Recommended Safety Practices

Allow only one child on the trampoline at a time, preventing collision injuries. Ensure the trampoline location has clear space above and around (8-10 feet) for movement. Regularly inspect the jumping surface, springs, and padding for wear. Secure the trampoline to ground anchors to prevent movement in wind.

Pre-Jump Warm-Up and Body Awareness

Encourage children to start slowly, letting the body adjust to the bouncing motion. Initially focus on basic vertical bouncing before attempting tricks. As confidence builds, children can develop more complex movements, but gradual progression prevents overconfidence-related injuries.
